TPA Insurance Empanelment

Expanding Healthcare Access Through Insurance Partnerships

What is TPA Insurance Empanelment?

In today's healthcare ecosystem, insurance-based treatment has become a major driver of hospital revenue. A large percentage of patients prefer hospitals that offer cashless treatment facilities under health insurance policies. To provide these services, hospitals must obtain empanelment with TPAs (Third Party Administrators) and insurance companies.

A Third Party Administrator (TPA) is an organization authorized by insurance companies to process claims and manage cashless hospitalization services. When a hospital is empanelled with TPAs and insurance companies, it can provide cashless treatment to insured patients, submit claims directly to TPAs, receive reimbursements as per agreed package rates, increase patient accessibility, and become part of insurance provider networks.

Scope of TPA Insurance Empanelment

TPA and insurance companies evaluate hospitals based on comprehensive criteria.

Infrastructure & Facilities

  • Hospital infrastructure and bed capacity
  • ICU and OT facilities
  • Biomedical waste management
  • Fire safety compliance

Operations & Compliance

  • Qualified medical staff
  • Equipment availability
  • Statutory licenses and registrations
  • NABH accreditation status (preferred)

Documentation & Systems

  • Billing and documentation systems
  • Patient record management
  • Claim processing protocols
  • Quality assurance measures
