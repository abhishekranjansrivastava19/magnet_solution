Introduction

Quality and patient safety have become critical pillars of healthcare delivery in India. With increasing patient awareness, regulatory expectations, and competition, hospitals and clinics must demonstrate compliance with recognized quality standards.

NABH Entry-Level Accreditation is designed as a practical and affordable starting point for healthcare organizations beginning their quality journey.

What is NABH Entry-Level Accreditation?

NABH Entry-Level Accreditation is a quality certification program introduced by the National Accreditation Board for Hospitals & Healthcare Providers (NABH) under the Quality Council of India (QCI). It focuses on basic patient safety standards, essential clinical practices, and statutory compliance.

Unlike NABH Full Accreditation, the Entry-Level program is simpler, cost-effective, and easier to implement while still maintaining strong quality principles. It serves as a foundation for healthcare organizations to gradually move toward higher levels of accreditation.

Who Should Apply for NABH Entry-Level Accreditation?

NABH Entry-Level Accreditation is ideal for:

  • Small and medium hospitals
  • Nursing homes
  • Day-care centers
  • Polyclinics and specialty clinics
  • Newly established healthcare facilities
  • Hospitals preparing for NABH Full Accreditation

This accreditation is especially suitable for organizations that want to improve systems without disrupting daily operations.

Scope of NABH Entry-Level Standards

NABH Entry-Level standards cover essential aspects of healthcare delivery, ensuring that patients receive safe, ethical, and quality healthcare services.

Patient Rights & Responsibilities

Ensuring patient dignity, privacy, and informed consent throughout the care process.

Infection Prevention & Control

Implementing protocols to minimize healthcare-associated infections and ensure a safe environment.

Medication Management

Safe prescribing, dispensing, administration and monitoring of medications to prevent errors.

Safe Clinical Practices

Standardized procedures for clinical care, surgical safety, and emergency response.

Facility Safety & Emergency Preparedness

Maintaining a safe physical environment and being prepared for emergencies.

Documentation & Record Keeping

Maintaining accurate, complete and timely patient and organizational records.
