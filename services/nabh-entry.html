NABH Entry Level Accreditation

National Accreditation Board for Hospitals & Healthcare Providers - Ensuring Quality Healthcare Standards Across India

What is NABH Entry Level Accreditation?

The NABH Entry Level Accreditation is a stepping stone for healthcare organizations in India to begin their quality improvement journey. It is designed for smaller hospitals and healthcare providers who are at the initial stages of implementing quality standards.

This accreditation focuses on building a strong foundation for patient safety and quality care by establishing basic protocols and systems. It serves as a preparatory stage for hospitals aspiring to achieve full NABH accreditation in the future.

Key Objective: To encourage healthcare organizations to implement basic quality and patient safety protocols, creating a culture of continuous improvement from the ground up.

Accreditation Process

The NABH Entry Level Accreditation process is designed to be achievable for smaller healthcare facilities while ensuring meaningful quality improvements.

1

Application

Healthcare organization submits formal application to NABH with required documents and fees.

2

Self-Assessment

Organization conducts internal assessment against Entry Level standards and identifies gaps.

3

Documentation

Preparation and implementation of required policies, procedures, and records as per standards.

4

Internal Audit

Organization conducts internal audit to verify implementation of quality systems.

5

Assessment

NABH conducts assessment through document review and on-site visit (if required).

6

Accreditation

Successful organizations receive Entry Level Accreditation valid for 2 years.

NABH accreditation involves thorough review of hospital processes and documentation

Note: The Entry Level Accreditation is valid for two years, during which the organization is expected to work towards full NABH accreditation. Organizations need to apply for full accreditation before the expiry of their Entry Level status.

Services Provided Under Accreditation

NABH Entry Level Accreditation ensures that healthcare organizations provide safe and quality services across all key areas.

Patient Care Services

  • Accessibility, assessment and continuity of care
  • Care of patients
  • Management of medication
  • Patient rights and education
  • Hospital infection control

Organization Management

  • Continuous quality improvement
  • Responsibilities of management
  • Facility management and safety
  • Human resource management
  • Information management system

Clinical Support Services

  • Management of laboratory services
  • Management of radiology services
  • Management of pharmacy services
  • Equipment management
  • Outpatient services

Importance of NABH Entry Level Accreditation

Achieving NABH Entry Level Accreditation provides significant benefits to healthcare organizations, patients, and the healthcare system as a whole.

Enhanced Patient Safety

Establishes protocols that reduce medical errors and healthcare-associated infections, ensuring safer care delivery.

Quality Improvement Culture

Fosters a systematic approach to quality, encouraging continuous monitoring and improvement of services.

Patient Confidence

Builds trust among patients and their families by demonstrating commitment to quality standards.

Staff Development

Improves staff morale and competency through training and clear protocols, reducing variability in care.

National Impact: NABH Entry Level Accreditation plays a crucial role in improving the overall quality of healthcare in India by making quality standards accessible to smaller hospitals and healthcare providers across the country, including in remote and rural areas.
